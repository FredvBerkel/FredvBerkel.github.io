Geological cross sections of the Cape Fold Belt

Explaining the evolution of the Cape Fold Belt to our Knysna U3A geology interest group during lectures and on field trips, is greatly facilitated by bringing out the three-dimentional nature of the crust using cross sections drawn across the Council of Geoscience 1:250K geology maps. Unfortunately, neither the maps nor the Explanatory booklets accompanying the maps, have cross sections.

Sections are drawn using Google Earth, 1:250K geology maps and PowerPoint. They are not balanced and have a >2x vertical exaggeration to bring out the structure of the fold belt.

The cross sections below were drawn as part of the information booklets prepared for the Knysna U3A geology group day trips and longer excursions.
